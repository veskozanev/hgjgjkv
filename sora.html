SORA: Virtual Health Clinic

SORA is a certified Class I Medical Device App. With SORA, you can track your symptoms and lifestyle, discover vital information about your cycle, access specialists 24/7 and share your experiences safely in a judgement-free zone. SORA was created by a team of scientists, clinicians, researchers and patients to create a platform which truly improves care pathways.

Project Details

Industry: m-Health, telemedicine

Project duration: 2 years

Deliverable: Ensure seamless UX/UI design

Design Team: Iliyana Pirinska, Sascha Bou Jaber

About SORA

You can use SORA to track and manage your health symptoms, get personalised health care plans; access specialist doctors (UK only), therapists, nutritionists; home-test kits (UK only), prescriptions (UK only), and medically approved articles and videos.
SORA can be personalised based on your condition, giving you an individualised experience. SORA helps you manage your symptoms, find patterns between your lifestyle and symptoms, and speak to specialists who understand.

My Responsibilities

As a UX/UI designer for the SORA app, my role involves a range of tasks to ensure that the app provides a seamless user experience for our users. One of my primary responsibilities is conducting UX research to better understand the needs and goals of our users. This includes tasks such as conducting interviews, usability testing, and questionnaires, which helps us gain insights into user behavior and preferences.

Based on the insights gathered from UX research, I collaborate with the development team to design the app's user interface (UI) with an emphasis on usability, accessibility, and user-centered design principles. This involves creating wireframes, prototyping, and UI design, considering the app's overall look and feel, typography, color palette, and iconography.

In addition to designing the UI, I also conduct industry design research to keep abreast of the latest design trends and to ensure that the app's design is modern and relevant. I also conduct accessibility audits to ensure that the app meets accessibility standards and can be used by people with disabilities.

To continually improve the app's UX/UI design, I conduct surveys, analyze user feedback and app usage data, and provide recommendations for design improvements. I also work closely with the development team to ensure that the design is implemented correctly and to address any issues that arise during development.

Overall, my work as a UX/UI designer for the SORA app is focused on creating a seamless user experience that helps users manage their health symptoms, connect with specialists, and access health-related resources with ease.



SORA features

1. Health Tracking

Everyone’s symptoms of the same condition vary. Hence, logging your symptoms and lifestyle data creates a digital blueprint of your condition that is personal to you. Have we missed a symptom? Don’t worry you can add yours anyway! We also have a handy notes function to help you journal effectively.

2. Care Plan

Monitor changes, and get personalised health care plans and recommendations based on your logged symptoms. Learn about important health patterns that can help you manage your symptoms.

3. Resources

Watch the latest videos to help you better manage your symptoms, and read medically approved content.


4. Support

Browse products and services recommended by SORA’s clinicians and users. Products include home testing kits, supplements and tools to help manage pain. Access specialists such as Doctors, Therapists and Nutritionists to support you.

5. Health Coach

If you’re stuck with what to do or you have simple questions about your health, you can chat with a health coach for advice, where you’ll be speaking to one of our registered nurses and physicians.
